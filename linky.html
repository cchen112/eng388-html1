Rosa the sea otter was well-known for her interactions with humans at the Monterey Bay Aquarium. Raised in the aquarium, she was a surrogate mother of 15 otters. She is older than the oldest male sea otter, Adaa, and gets her physical checkups 3 times a year. Her favorite food was shells and crabs which were delivered using a plastic ball. After some time, she retired and was released into the wild in 2019.
